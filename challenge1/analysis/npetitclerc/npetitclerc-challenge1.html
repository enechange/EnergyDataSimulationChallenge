Intuitively it seems like the most important parameters/dimensions should be: House, Month, Temperature and Daylight. But to be sure (maybe the data were tricked??) I did a univariate feature selection. I did a SVM regression and looked at the weight of the individual parameters.

# Univariate feature selection with F-test for feature scoring
selector = feature_selection.SelectPercentile(feature_selection.f_regression, percentile=60)
selector.fit(x_train, y_train)
 
clf = svm.SVR(kernel='linear')
clf.fit(x_train, y_train)
svm_weights = (clf.coef_ ** 2).sum(axis=0)
svm_weights /= svm_weights.max()
 
clf_selected = svm.SVR(kernel='linear')
clf_selected.fit(selector.transform(x_train), y_train)
svm_weights_selected = (clf_selected.coef_ ** 2).sum(axis=0)
svm_weights_selected /= svm_weights_selected.max()

We can see the Energy production in function of the individual parameters with the fitted slope in red. Temperature and Daylight are fitted fine, and clearly Month is not linear. But the real problem here is with Houses, they aren't sorted in any particular order, so no trend is found in the data. I tried to sort the houses with their averaged Energy production and that improved the fit a lot. But sorting is still just an approximation, so I added a parameter to the data "Mean Energy Production" which is the average energy production of that house. Then I substracted this value to the Energy production to get just the variance. That improved the fit quite a bit as well.
